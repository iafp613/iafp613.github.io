About me

Hi! My name is Nacho Fontal. I am a Data Scientist. After finishing my nursing degree in Andalusia, I dedicated my time to caring for people. Thanks to this I was able to learn a lot about empathy, assertiveness, human fragility and its strength.

Years later, due to personal circumstances, I moved to the north of Spain. Due to lack of job opportunities, I started working on one of my passions: cooking.

Starting from scratch and absorbing all the knowledge and experience that I was surrounded by, I was able to rise step by step until I became head chef, and even head chef of a group of several restaurants.

job gave me the skills to work in a team, learn self-sufficiently, teach inexperienced coworkers, and withstand situations of maximum stress.

Then, taking advantage of the hospitality break due to the Covid-19 pandemic, I took the opportunity to reorient my future and learn about what I always wanted: technology. I studied self-taught for 1 year and later did a Data Science bootcamp.

Now, eager for knowledge, I am looking for the perfect job opportunity to be able to contribute my skills and develop in this infinite world of data science. Do you want to know more about me?

Spain's mortality

Project Brief:

Over the last few years, medical and technological advances, robotization, the improvement of medicine and technological research have brought about an increase in life expectancy. On the contrary, massive migration to cities and their stressful pace of life, pollution, food laden with chemicals and globalization have been determining factors in the proliferation of new diseases. That is why the question arises as to whether the mortality rate is increasing or decreasing with the advancement of science. With an in-depth study of mortality and its causes in the last 40 years, we will be able to deduce where we are going in the future.
